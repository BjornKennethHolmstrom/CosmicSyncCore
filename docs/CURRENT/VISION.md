## The Problem: Digital Sovereignty at Risk

Sweden's digital infrastructure has three critical dependencies:

### 1. **Internet Backbone Vulnerability**
- Single points of failure (undersea cables, ISP backbones)
- November 2024: C-Lion1 and BCS East-West cable cuts demonstrated risk
- Current municipal systems: 100% cloud-dependent, 0% offline capability
- Recovery time: Unknown, depends on American/European infrastructure

### 2. **Cloud Service Lock-In**
- 95%+ of Swedish municipal IT runs on AWS, Azure, or Google Cloud
- All data stored outside Sweden's borders
- Service continuity requires uninterrupted internet access
- Geopolitical de-platforming risk (sanctions, political pressure)

### 3. **Economic Centralization**
- Payment systems (Swish/BankID) require central servers + internet
- 95% cashless society = total vulnerability during disruptions
- Local communities cannot maintain economic activity during crises
- Care economy (elder care, childcare, community services) becomes impossible

## Our Technical Approach

### Peer-to-Peer Foundation (libp2p)
- Battle-tested technology (powers IPFS, Filecoin globally)
- No central servers = no single point of failure
- NAT traversal for global connectivity
- Efficient mesh networking when internet fails

### Agent-Centric Data Model
- Each user controls their own data
- Cryptographic identity (no central database to compromise)
- Granular sharing permissions
- GDPR-compliant deletion (data actually deleted, not just flagged)

### Offline-First Synchronization
- All operations work offline by default
- Conflict resolution via last-write-wins + vector clocks
- Sync happens automatically when connectivity returns
- Configurable sync strategies per application

### Security by Design
- End-to-end encryption (AES-256-GCM)
- No plaintext data in transit or at rest
- Agent-centric identity (decentralized, not federated)
- Regular security audits (funded in grant)
