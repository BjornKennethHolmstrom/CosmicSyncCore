# CommonsNet Vision

> *Coordinating our shared resources for human flourishing*

## The Vision

**CommonsNet is the flagship application for a new societal operating system** - a decentralized platform that enables communities to coordinate labor, share resources, and manage collective assets in ways that prioritize human well-being over mere efficiency.

We're building the practical infrastructure for a world where:
- **Tractors are shared** among farmers rather than sitting idle 90% of the time
- **Neighborhood tools circulate** freely instead of gathering dust in individual garages
- **Labor matches meaningful work** based on skills and purpose, not just market demand
- **Communities govern their commons** through transparent, participatory systems
- **Resource allocation serves human flourishing**, not just economic growth

## The Problem: Our Broken Coordination Systems

### The Tragedy of the Commons 2.0
Traditional commons failed because we lacked the technology for:
- **Transparent tracking** of resource usage
- **Dynamic allocation** based on real needs
- **Accountability systems** for shared maintenance
- **Participatory governance** at scale

### The Limitations of Markets
Markets excel at pricing scarcity but fail at:
- **Valuing community well-being** over individual profit
- **Accounting for environmental costs**
- **Preventing wealth concentration**
- **Fostering cooperation over competition**

### Digital Platform Failures
Modern platforms create value but capture it for shareholders:
- **Uber/Lyft**: Coordinate rides but extract 25-40% of value
- **Airbnb**: Enable sharing but drive up housing costs
- **TaskRabbit**: Connect labor but create precarious work

## Core Principles

### 1. **Subsidiarity in Action**
Decisions made at the most local level possible, with higher-level coordination only when necessary.

### 2. **Pluralistic Value Recognition**
Multiple forms of value recognized: economic, social, ecological, spiritual.

### 3. **Dynamic Resource Flow**  
Resources circulate based on real-time needs, not static ownership.

### 4. **Participatory Governance**
Those affected by decisions participate in making them.

### 5. **Graceful Degradation**
System remains functional even when parts fail or connectivity is lost.
